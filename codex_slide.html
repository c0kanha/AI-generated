How LLMs Work

Pattern prediction at scale, with useful reasoning-like behavior.

What Is True

  • LLMs are trained to predict the next token from massive text and code datasets
  • That objective can produce strong capabilities in writing, coding, summarization, translation, and many multi-step tasks
  • They can often simulate planning and reasoning well enough to be practically useful
  • They work best when combined with tools, retrieval, and human review

What Is Not Guaranteed

  • Fluent output is not the same as truth, understanding, or reliability
  • Models can hallucinate facts, citations, calculations, or explanations
  • Performance on benchmarks does not guarantee expert-level performance in real-world settings
  • Uncertainty and refusal behavior depend heavily on training, prompting, and system design

LLMs are fundamentally prediction systems, yet their scale makes them surprisingly capable. They can be powerful reasoning aids, but they should not be treated as automatically correct or self-verifying.

Useful, impressive, and fallible.
